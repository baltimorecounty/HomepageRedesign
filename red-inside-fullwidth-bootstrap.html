  • Baltimore County Center for Maryland Agriculture and Farm Park

    About this Site

    The approximately 149-acre Baltimore County Center for Maryland Agriculture and Farm Park is dedicated to interpreting agricultural practices and their relationship with the natural environment. The park features trails, an agricultural resource center and a variety of intepretive areas.

    Contact Information
    Baltimore County Ag. Center Office, baltcoagcenter@baltimorecountymd.gov
    Address: 1114 Shawan Road, Cockeysville 21030
    Phone: 410-887-8572

    • Banneker Historical Park and Museum

      About this Site

      The approximately 127-acre Banneker Historical Park and Museum features numerous trails, a museum, interpretive facilities and areas including a reproduction period cabin, a gazebo, and picnic grove. Parking is available by the museum, and trail access is to the rear of the building.

      Site Map
      Contact Information
      Banneker Historical Park Office
      Address: 300 Oella Avenue, Catonsville 21228
      Phone: 410-887-1081
      Email: bannekermuseum-rp@baltimorecountymd.gov
      • Featured Trail

      Banneker Historical Park - Mile Loop Trail

      The one-mile loop trail starts to the rear of the park's museum, and follows parts of the Heritage Loop, Ice Pond, Ridge, and Nature trails, as well as a portion of the Number Nine Trolley Line Trail. Sections of the trail traverse steep slopes. See trail map for details about the loop trail's route.

      Type: Mostly Natural

      Trail Map

    • Belmont Park

      About this Site

      The approximately 48-acre Belmont Park is a community park featuring paths and nature trails, ball fields, picnic pavilions (available for reservation through the recreation office) and playground. Access to the start of the loop route is available where the access road and parking bend to the right.

      Site Map
      Contact Information
      Parkville Recreation & Parks Office
      Address: 8701 Walther Boulevard, Parkville 21236
      Phone: 410-887-5300
      Email: parkville-rp@baltimorecountymd.gov
      • Featured Trail

      Belmont Park - Loop Path

      A loop circuit of just over 1/2-mile is displayed on the available map. Start at the end of the path adjoing the long entry road and parking lot, just before it turns to the right. Please exhibit caution when sports activities are underway on the adjacent ball fields.

      Type: Paved and Grass

    • Carrollwood Manor Local Open Space - Nollmeyer Road

      About this Site

      Carrollwood Manor Local Open Space - Nollmeyer Road is a 5-acre neighborhood recreation site in the Carroll Island area of Middle River. The park features a pond with an encircling path of multiple surface types, including a section that runs along the road’s edge. Nearby street parking is available.

      Site Map
      Contact Information
      Bengies-Chase Recreation & Parks Office,
      Address: 706 Nollmeyer Road, Middle River, 21220
      Phone: 410-887-5194
      • Featured Trail

      Carrollwood Manor Open Space - Nollmeyer Road

      Loop path, accessed from Nollmeyer Road approximately opposite Tidewood Road. The loop, which circles a pond and runs partially along the road, is just under 1/4 of a mile in length. Parking is along the road, as there is no site parking lot.

      Type: Mostly Paved

    • Catonsville Community Park

      About this Site

      The approximately 40-acre Catonsville Community Park and Senior Center features a variety of recreational facilities including nature trails, ball fields, tennis courts, sand volleyball courts, picnic tables, and a playground. Parking access to the nature trails is situated by the playground.

      Site Map
      Contact Information
      Catonsville Recreation & Parks Office
      Address: 501 N. Rolling Road, Catonsville 21228
      Phone: 410-887-0959
      Email: catonsville-rp@baltimorecountymd.gov
      • Featured Trail

      Catonsville Community Park - Loop Trails

      A loop route of approximately 0.8 milesis displayed on the site map. Some sections of this route have steep slopes, and a portion that runs atop the earthen dam is repeated on the return route. Many other trail routes area available, but the trails at the park are not formally marked and named.

      Type: Mostly Natural
